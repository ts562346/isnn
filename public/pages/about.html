About ISNNS

Islamic Society of Nigerians in Nova Scotia

Who we are

We are Nigerians residing in Nova Scotia who share a profound love for Islam and a strong sense of responsibility toward its preservation and propagation. United by faith and a common heritage, we are committed to upholding the teachings of Islam in our daily lives while fostering a supportive and spiritually enriching community for our members.

Our collective aim is not only to strengthen our own understanding and practice of the religion, but also to actively transmit its values, principles, and teachings to the next generation. Through continuous learning, community engagement, and the organization of Islamic programs and activities, we strive to nurture an environment where our children and future generations can grow with a firm foundation in the Qur'an and Sunnah, embodying sound character and a strong Islamic identity.

How we started

In 2018, in recognition of the need to deepen our understanding of Islam and enrich ourselves with beneficial knowledge, a small group of families came together with the sincere intention of seeking the pleasure of Allah (SWT). We began by engaging in regular gatherings of remembrance (adhkar), supplication, and mutual encouragement upon righteousness and piety. These early efforts, though modest, were rooted in sincerity and a shared desire to grow spiritually and uphold the teachings of Islam in their daily lives.

By the grace of Allah, what began as a small circle gradually expanded, as more families and individuals were drawn to the initiative. The gatherings became a means of fostering unity, brotherhood, and sisterhood, while also serving as a platform for learning and spiritual upliftment. Participants benefited from collective remembrance, informal knowledge sharing, and a supportive environment that encouraged adherence to Islamic values.

In November 2020, in order to provide structure, sustainability, and a broader scope for our activities, the group was formally established and registered as a non-profit organization. This marked a significant milestone in its development, enabling it to organize more structured programs, outreach initiatives, and educational activities. Since then, the Society has continued to grow steadily, welcoming Muslims from across Nova Scotia. Today, it stands as a vibrant and diverse community united by a common purpose: to strengthen faith, increase authentic Islamic knowledge, and nurture future generations upon the guidance of the Qur'an and Sunnah.

Our Mission

To promote the understanding and practice of Islam among members through regular Islamic lectures, educational programs, and community engagement, fostering spiritual growth, unity, and moral excellence. We strive to equip our children and future generations with sound Islamic knowledge and character, guided by the Qur'an and Sunnah, so they may live as confident and responsible Muslims in society.

Our Vision

To nurture a united and spiritually grounded Nigerian Muslim community in Nova Scotia, devoted to the teachings of Islam, exemplifying righteousness, and raising future generations firmly rooted in authentic Islamic values and knowledge.

Guided by the Qur'an and Sunnah, we build faith, unity, and sound character.
